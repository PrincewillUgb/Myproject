The Light Roast

Light roast coffee beans are roasted for a shorter duration, resulting in a lighter color and preserving more of their original flavors and characteristics. They have a crisp acidity, subtle fruity or floral notes, and a milder taste compared to darker roasts. The beans have a dry surface and are often preferred by those who enjoy the distinct flavors of different coffee varieties.

The Middle Roast

Medium roast coffee beans are roasted for a slightly longer time than light roasts but still retain many of their original flavors. They have a balanced acidity and a pleasant aroma with more pronounced caramel or nutty undertones. Medium roasts strike a middle ground between the delicate taste of light roasts and the richer flavors of dark roasts, making them popular among a wide range of coffee enthusiasts.

The Dark Roast

Dark roast coffee beans undergo a longer roasting process, leading to a darker color, oilier surface, and a more robust flavor profile. The beans have a bittersweet taste, often characterized by chocolate, toasted, or smoky notes. Dark roasts have a reduced acidity and may exhibit a fuller body, making them popular for espresso preparations and among those who prefer a stronger, bold coffee flavor.
